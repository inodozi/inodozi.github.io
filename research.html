I study Measure-valued Proximal Recursions for Learning and Control. I investigate convex optimization problems over the space of probability measures. The overarching goal of my research is to investigate convex optimization problems over the space of probability measures. Several problems in machine learning and statistics can be cast in this form. This includes sampling from an unnormalized prior, policy iteration in reinforcement learning, mean-field dynamics of classification, and Wasserstein Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) via variants of stochastic gradient descent. Several problems in control theory can also be cast in this form. Exemplars include prediction and nonlinear estimation of conditional joint state distributions, optimal distribution steering a.k.a. Schrodinger bridge, and its zero-noise limit: optimal mass transport.
